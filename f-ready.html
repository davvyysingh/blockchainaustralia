The Problem
Your PDF invoice will
become illegal.
The UAE is enforcing Fatoora — mandatory structured e-invoicing. Every invoice must be a PEPPOL-compliant XML file cleared by the FTA in real time. Most businesses have no idea how to do this.
AED 500K
Maximum fine for non-compliant invoicing
  • Issuing a PDF invoice instead of PEPPOL XML
  • Failing to transmit invoices to FTA in real time
  • Missing mandatory fields (UUID, hash, QR code)
  • Not archiving e-invoices for 5 years in approved format
  • Late VAT filing due to incorrect invoice records
ERP systems cost AED 50,000+ to implement
SAP, Oracle, and Microsoft Dynamics all support Fatoora — if you have a 6-month implementation budget and a dedicated IT team.
Zoho and QuickBooks aren't UAE-localised yet
Popular SME accounting tools have not yet released PEPPOL modules specific to UAE FTA requirements. You can't wait for them.
Consultants charge AED 5,000–20,000 per setup
Compliance consultants will set you up once — but every invoice you issue, every VAT period, every format change is back on you.
The deadline doesn't move
Cabinet Resolution No. (106) of 2025 is in effect. Phased rollout by business size is already underway. Your phase date may be sooner than you think.
