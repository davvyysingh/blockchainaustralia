FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from founders considering a Dubai entity.

How much does it cost to set up a company in Dubai?
From AED 15,000–50,000 depending on the free zone and license type. We provide a fixed-cost quote upfront — no surprises mid-process. The variation is driven primarily by free zone fees and the number of visa allocations required.
Can I open a bank account for a crypto business in Dubai?
Yes — with the right structure and documentation. We have a 90%+ banking approval rate for crypto businesses. The key is matching your business activity to the correct free zone and preparing a compliance package that banks are comfortable with. Generic approaches fail here; our relationships and preparation do not.
Do I need to be physically present in Dubai?
For most free zones, no. We handle everything remotely using notarised and apostilled documents. Certain bank accounts may require a single in-person visit, which we will advise you about upfront. The majority of our clients complete the entire process without visiting Dubai.
Which free zone is best for a crypto exchange or DeFi project?
DMCC is most commonly recommended for crypto exchanges and DeFi projects due to its Virtual Asset license and established banking relationships. DIFC is preferred for regulated FinTech with institutional clients. We assess your specific business model before making a recommendation — the wrong zone is costly to change.
How long does the full process take?
Our average is 21 days from engagement to a live company with banking approved. Company incorporation typically takes 5–10 working days; banking approval adds a further 5–14 days depending on the bank. We set a realistic timeline at the outset and keep you updated at every step.
