FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions we hear most before founders book their strategy call.

Which banks accept crypto companies in Dubai?
We share this on our strategy call — not publicly, to protect our relationships with those institutions. Publishing it would cause them to be flooded with unqualified applications, which would erode the very relationships that get our clients approved. What we can tell you: we have active approvals with both tier-1 UAE banks and licensed EMIs.
What if my application is rejected?
We have backup options. We submit to 2–3 institutions simultaneously, which means that a single rejection rarely leaves you without banking. Our 90%+ success rate reflects that very few clients end up without at least one active account. In the rare case of difficulty, we have EMI pathways that offer same-day IBAN and multi-currency functionality.
Is an EMI account suitable for my business?
Often yes — modern licensed EMIs offer multi-currency accounts, dedicated IBAN, SWIFT access, and same-day setup. For many Web3 and crypto businesses, an EMI paired with a traditional account offers more flexibility than a traditional bank alone. We assess your specific transaction volumes, counterparties, and business model before advising on fit.
We've already been rejected by two banks. Can you still help?
Yes — this is actually a common situation. Prior rejections create a compliance record, but they don't disqualify you from approval. We've helped companies with 3+ prior rejections get approved within 19 days. The key is using the right institutions and the right compliance narrative from the start.
Do I need to be physically present in Dubai for the banking process?
Some tier-1 banks require an in-person meeting — we will advise you of this upfront and can coordinate the visit. EMI accounts can typically be approved and activated fully remotely. We tell you exactly what to expect before you engage so there are no surprises.
